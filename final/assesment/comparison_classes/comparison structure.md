# Comparison Structure

## Hero Image

### Proposal Opportunities

Proposal opportunity measures the volume of space where a proposal becomes possible.

Examples:

- `Lizards propose to Colonise building envelopes across 9 times more roof, facade and ground surface area.`
- `Trees propose Decay across 10 times more space.`

Human Pathway Examples:

- `Lizards propose to Colonise building envelopes across one-ninth the roof, facade and ground surface area.`
- `Trees propose Decay across one-tenth the space.`

### Community Response

Community response measures how much of that proposal space communities fully support, partially support, or refuse through interventions.

Examples:

- `Communities withdraw pruning from 79.4% of canopy that Birds, Lizards and Trees propose for release from control.`
- `The community fully supports Decay across 10 times more space where ageing trees can senesce and collapse in place.`
- `Where humans need safe walking paths under canopies, the community braces branches on 5 times more ageing trees.`

Human Pathway Examples:

- `Humans withdraw pruning from only 22.3% of canopy that Birds, Lizards and Trees propose for release from control.`
- `Humans fully support Decay across one-tenth the space where ageing trees can senesce and collapse in place.`
- `Humans partially support Decay across one-fifth the ageing trees that communities brace in place.`

### Implementation

Implementation measures how those interventions modify urban elements, canopy control conditions, or insert additional structures within that space.

Examples:

- `The community converts 8 times more roofs and ground into insect-attracting grasslands where structures can carry the load.`
- `On roofs and facades that are too weak or steep for full conversion, the community makes 10 times more surfaces traversable for Lizard mate-searching with scattered rocks and small logs.`
- `The community converts 23 times more road space into safe collapse zones for ageing trees.`
- `The community converts 10 times more road space into suitable conditions for saplings to emerge.`
- `Communities move fallen trees onto rooftops, creating 4 times more sheltered refuge for Lizards.`
- `Withdrawing pruning creates 6 times more hollows, 5 times more perchable canopy, and 5 times more peeling bark in low-control canopy.`

Human Pathway Examples:

- `Humans leave roofs and ground unconverted, limiting insect-attracting grasslands to one-eighth the space communities create.`
- `Humans leave roofs and facades bare.`
- `Humans allocate one-twenty-third the space for ageing trees to collapse and return biomass to the ground.`
- `Humans keep road space paved, limiting suitable conditions for saplings to one-tenth the space communities convert.`
- `Humans keep fallen trees off rooftops, limiting sheltered refuge for Lizards to one-quarter the space communities create.`
- `Humans replace decaying trees with young saplings and aggressively prune their canopies.`
- `Maintaining pruning leaves hollows, perchable canopy, and peeling bark at one-sixth, one-fifth, and one-fifth the nonhuman-led rate.`

### Indicator Outcomes

Indicator outcomes measure the opportunities those changes create for personas to express capabilities, reported as totals and relative to baseline.

Examples:

- `Community support for Colonise restores foraging for Lizards to 98% of baseline through rooftops planted with shrubs and deadwood.`
- `Community support for Colonise lifts connectivity for Lizards to 165% of baseline through roughened roofs and facades.`
- `Community support for Colonise lifts refuge space for Lizards to 27% of baseline through dead trees returned to rooftops.`
- `Community support for Recruit expands recruitment for Trees to 52% of baseline through depaved ground near canopy.`
- `Loamy soil conditions reconnect Trees through fungal networks, lifting communication to 45.1% of baseline.`

Human Pathway Examples:

- `Human-led management leaves foraging for Lizards at 19.8% of baseline by withholding shrubs and deadwood from rooftops.`
- `Human-led management leaves connectivity for Lizards at 24.5% of baseline by keeping roofs and facades bare.`
- `Human-led management leaves refuge space for Lizards at 5.4% of baseline by keeping dead trees off rooftops.`
- `Human-led management leaves recruitment for Trees at 8.0% of baseline by keeping ground near canopy paved.`
- `Compacted ground leaves Tree communication at 11.0% of baseline by preventing fungal networks from reconnecting.`
