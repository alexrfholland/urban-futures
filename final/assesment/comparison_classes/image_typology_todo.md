## Where We Are At Now

### Core Distinction

- Some figures prioritise `abstraction`.
- Some prioritise `spatial distribution`.
- Some prioritise `time`.
- Some prioritise `experiential condition`.

### Three Scales

- `Habitat volume scale`: site-wide quantities, distributions, clustering, and connectivity.
- `Fine scale`: habitat features and capability-linked geometries.
- `Hybrid scale`: enough detail for the reader to understand local and material consequences.

### Current Short Definitions

- To prioritise `spatial distribution`: use habitat volume images to show where conditions occur across the site.
- To prioritise `time`: stratify comparable site views across pathway assessment years.
- To prioritise `experiential condition`: use hybrid images, sometimes with fine-scale detail, to show what predicted outcomes mean on the ground.

### Current Working Paragraph

The manuscript uses three spatial image types: habitat volume, fine scale, and hybrid scale. Habitat volume images show site-wide quantities and distributions; fine-scale images show habitat features; and hybrid images show enough site detail to understand local and material consequences. We prioritise spatial distribution through habitat volume views, time through stratified pathway views across years, and experiential condition through hybrid images with selected fine-scale detail.
