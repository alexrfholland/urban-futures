## Explanation of indicator divergence at Year 180

This summary uses three divergence classes: minimal divergence for values under 2x, moderate divergence for 2x to under 5x, and large divergence for 5x or more, or where the trending pathway is effectively zero.

For readability, reported percentages below 0.1% are shown as 0.0%.

### Parade

#### Bird / Acquire Resources. Large divergence (5.34x, 45.6% vs 8.5%).

Positive keeps more low-control canopy, so peeling bark stays clearly ahead of trending.

#### Bird / Communicate. Minimal divergence (1.23x, 25.5% vs 20.7%).

Perchable canopy remains common in both paths, so the separation stays modest.

#### Bird / Reproduce. Large divergence (134.22x, 57.3% vs 0.4%).

Positive still holds the hollow stock; trending drops close to zero, so the gap is much stronger than before.

#### Lizard / Acquire Resources. Large divergence (32.91x, 100.8% vs 3.1%).

Positive retains deadwood and epiphytes, and the combined gap is much larger than before.

#### Lizard / Communicate. Moderate divergence (2.61x, 134.3% vs 51.4%).

Open ground stays broad in both paths, but positive still keeps more non-paved surface.

#### Lizard / Reproduce. Large divergence (∞x, 68.8% vs 0.0%).

Positive retains fallen-tree habitat; trending falls to zero.

#### Tree / Acquire Resources. Large divergence (∞x, 97.2% vs 0.0%).

Senescent biovolume stays exclusive to the positive path.

#### Tree / Communicate. Moderate divergence (3.04x, 133.9% vs 44.0%).

Canopy-ground overlap remains present in both paths, but positive is still ahead.

#### Tree / Reproduce. Large divergence (4896.81x, 111.8% vs 0.0%).

Positive keeps the recruitment grassland stock and trending stays near zero.
